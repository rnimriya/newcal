While converting a fraction to a decimal requires long division, converting a decimal back into a fraction requires a deep understanding of **place value**. 

If you are following a recipe that calls for `0.75` cups of flour, your measuring cups won't help you because they are labeled in fractions. Knowing how to convert decimals back into fractions is a critical life skill for cooking, carpentry, and basic accounting.

## The Place Value Method

The easiest way to convert a terminating decimal (a decimal that does not repeat infinitely) into a fraction is to use the Base-10 place value system.

Every digit to the right of a decimal point has a specific mathematical name based on its position:
- 1st position: Tenths (1/10)
- 2nd position: Hundredths (1/100)
- 3rd position: Thousandths (1/1,000)
- 4th position: Ten-Thousandths (1/10,000)

**Step 1: Write the decimal as a fraction over its place value.**
Let's convert the decimal **0.65**.
- The number 5 is in the second position (the hundredths place).
- Therefore, write the number 65 over 100.
`Fraction: 65 / 100`

**Step 2: Simplify the fraction.**
While 65/100 is technically correct, it is not mathematically simplified. You must find the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) for both the top and the bottom.
- Both 65 and 100 can be divided by 5.
- `65 ÷ 5 = 13`
- `100 ÷ 5 = 20`
**Final Simplified Fraction: 13/20**

## Converting Whole Numbers and Decimals

What if the number has a digit to the left of the decimal point, like **2.35**? 

This is called a **Mixed Number**. 
1. Ignore the whole number (2) for a moment.
2. Convert the decimal (0.35) using the place value method.
   - The 5 is in the hundredths place.
   - `35 / 100`
3. Simplify the fraction by dividing by 5.
   - `7 / 20`
4. Put the whole number back in front.
**Final Answer: 2 7/20**

## The Nightmare of Repeating Decimals

Converting a repeating decimal (like `0.333333...`) into a fraction is much harder, because there is no final "place value" to put the number over. It goes on to infinity.

To solve this manually, you have to use a complex algebra trick involving `x`.
**Example: Convert 0.333... to a fraction.**
1. Let `x = 0.333...`
2. Because 1 digit is repeating, multiply both sides by 10. 
   `10x = 3.333...`
3. Subtract the original `x` from `10x`.
   `10x = 3.333...`
  `- 1x = 0.333...`
   **Result:** `9x = 3`
4. Solve for x.
   `x = 3/9`
5. Simplify the fraction.
**Final Answer: 1/3**

## FAQ

**Why do we need to simplify fractions?**
In mathematics, simplifying fractions provides a standardized way to compare numbers. `500/1000` and `1/2` mean the exact same thing, but `1/2` is universally understood instantly without requiring the brain to process large numbers.
