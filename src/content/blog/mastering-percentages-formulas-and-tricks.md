The word "percentage" comes from the Latin *per centum*, meaning "by a hundred." A percentage is simply a fraction with a denominator of 100. Whether you are calculating a tip at a restaurant, figuring out how much a 30% off sale actually saves you, or analyzing a company's profit margin, percentages are everywhere.

## The Three Core Percentage Formulas

There are three main types of percentage problems you will encounter in daily life.

### 1. Finding X% of Y
*Example: What is 20% of $150?*
**Formula:** `(X / 100) * Y`
**Calculation:** (20 / 100) * 150 = 0.20 * 150 = **$30**

### 2. Finding what percentage X is of Y
*Example: 45 is what percent of 60?*
**Formula:** `(X / Y) * 100`
**Calculation:** (45 / 60) * 100 = 0.75 * 100 = **75%**

### 3. Finding Percentage Increase or Decrease
*Example: A stock went from $80 to $100. What is the percentage increase?*
**Formula:** `[(New Value - Old Value) / |Old Value|] * 100`
**Calculation:** [(100 - 80) / 80] * 100 = (20 / 80) * 100 = **25% Increase**

## Mental Math Tricks for Percentages

You don't always need a calculator. Here are the fastest ways to calculate percentages in your head:

1. **The 10% Trick:** To find 10% of any number, simply move the decimal point one place to the left. 
   *(10% of 45.0 = 4.5)*
2. **The 1% Trick:** To find 1% of any number, move the decimal point two places to the left. 
   *(1% of 450 = 4.5)*
3. **The 20% Tip:** Move the decimal one place to the left to find 10%, then double it! 
   *(Bill is $80 -> 10% is $8 -> 20% tip is $16)*

### The Reversible Percentage Trick
Many people don't realize that percentages are reversible! 
**X% of Y is exactly the same as Y% of X.**

*Example:* What is 16% of 50? That sounds hard to do in your head. 
Flip it: What is 50% of 16? It's exactly **8**.

## Profit Margin vs. Markup

In business, people often confuse markup with profit margin. They are not the same!

| Metric | Definition | Formula | Example (Cost=$10, Sell=$15) |
|--------|------------|---------|------------------------------|
| **Markup** | The percentage added to the cost to get the selling price. | `(Profit / Cost) * 100` | ($5 / $10) * 100 = **50% Markup** |
| **Margin** | The percentage of the selling price that is profit. | `(Profit / Revenue) * 100` | ($5 / $15) * 100 = **33.3% Margin** |

*A 50% markup only yields a 33.3% profit margin.*

## FAQ

**How do I add tax to a purchase?**
To quickly calculate the total price including tax, add the tax percentage to 100% and multiply. If the item is $50 and tax is 8%, multiply `$50 x 1.08 = $54`.
