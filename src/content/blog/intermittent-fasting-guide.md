## What is Intermittent Fasting?

Intermittent fasting (IF) has become one of the world's most popular health and fitness trends. Unlike a traditional diet that restricts *what* you eat, IF restricts *when* you eat. It involves cycling between periods of fasting and eating.

By giving your body a prolonged break from digesting food, intermittent fasting can lower insulin levels, increase Human Growth Hormone (HGH), and trigger important cellular repair processes like autophagy.

## The Popular 16:8 Method

The 16:8 method involves fasting every day for 16 hours and restricting your daily eating window to 8 hours. 

For most people, this schedule is the easiest to maintain because a large portion of the fast happens while you sleep. For example, if you finish your last meal at 8:00 PM, you simply skip breakfast the next day and eat your first meal at 12:00 PM (Noon).

### How to Calculate Your Eating Window

Figuring out exactly when you can start and stop eating can be confusing if you work odd shifts or change your schedule.

The math behind it is simple:
$$ \text{Eating Window} = 24 - \text{Fasting Hours} $$
$$ \text{Break Fast Time} = (\text{Last Meal Time} + \text{Fasting Hours}) \pmod{24} $$

## Benefits of Intermittent Fasting

1. **Weight Loss:** By eating fewer meals, IF can lead to an automatic reduction in calorie intake.
2. **Insulin Resistance:** IF can reduce insulin resistance, lowering blood sugar by 3-6% and fasting insulin levels by 20-31%.
3. **Brain Health:** IF increases the brain hormone BDNF and may aid the growth of new nerve cells.
