Of all the mathematical conversions required in daily life—fractions to decimals, Celsius to Fahrenheit, miles to kilometers—converting a decimal to a percentage is by far the easiest. It requires no long division and no complex memorization.

You just need to understand what the word "percent" actually means.

## The Meaning of "Percent"

The word "percent" is derived from the Latin phrase *per centum*, which literally translates to **"by a hundred"** or **"out of a hundred."**

A percentage is simply a specific type of fraction where the denominator (the bottom number) is always 100.
- 50% literally means 50 out of 100 (50/100).
- 5% literally means 5 out of 100 (5/100).

When we use decimals, the Base-10 system already builds this "out of 100" logic into the place values. The second digit to the right of the decimal point is the "hundredths" place. 

## The Rule: Move the Decimal Two Places to the Right

To convert any decimal into a percentage, you simply **multiply the decimal by 100** and add a `%` sign.

Because our number system is Base-10, multiplying any number by 100 is identical to physically moving the decimal point exactly two places to the right.

### Example 1: Standard Decimals
Convert **0.75** to a percentage.
1. Move the decimal point one place: `7.5`
2. Move it a second place: `75.`
**Answer: 75%**

### Example 2: Decimals Smaller Than One Percent
Convert **0.005** to a percentage.
1. Move the decimal point one place: `0.05`
2. Move it a second place: `0.5`
**Answer: 0.5%** *(This is half of one percent).*

### Example 3: Decimals Larger Than One
Convert **2.5** to a percentage.
1. Move the decimal point one place: `25.`
2. Move it a second place (you must add a zero to act as a placeholder): `250.`
**Answer: 250%**

## Why Do We Add Zeros?

If you try to convert the decimal `1.4` into a percentage, moving the decimal two places to the right gives you an empty space: `14_`. 

In mathematics, you can add an infinite number of zeros to the right of a terminating decimal without changing its value. `1.4` is exactly the same as `1.4000`. So, when you move the decimal point two places to the right on `1.40`, you clearly get **140%**.

## FAQ

**How do I convert a Percentage back to a Decimal?**
You just reverse the process! Instead of multiplying by 100, you divide by 100. Visually, you drop the `%` sign and move the decimal point two places to the **left**. 
*(e.g., 45% becomes 0.45).*
