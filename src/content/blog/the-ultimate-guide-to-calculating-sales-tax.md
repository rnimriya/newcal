If you live in the United States, you know the frustration of looking at a price tag for $9.99 and being asked to pay $10.84 at the register. Unlike many countries that use a Value-Added Tax (VAT) system where the tax is included in the advertised price, American retailers add sales tax at the point of sale.

## The Sales Tax Formula

Calculating sales tax is a straightforward percentage math problem.

**Sales Tax Amount = Price × (Tax Rate / 100)**
**Total Price = Price + Sales Tax Amount**

Alternatively, you can calculate the Total Price in a single step by multiplying the original price by 1 plus the tax rate as a decimal.
**Total Price = Price × (1 + (Tax Rate / 100))**

### Example Calculation
You are buying a laptop for $1,200 in a city with an 8.5% sales tax rate.
1. Convert 8.5% to a decimal: `8.5 / 100 = 0.085`
2. Multiply by the price: `$1,200 × 0.085 = $102`
3. Add to the original price: `$1,200 + $102 = $1,302`

Your total cost at the register is **$1,302**.

## How to Reverse Calculate Sales Tax

Sometimes you only have the final receipt and want to know how much the item cost before tax. This requires reverse calculation.

**Pre-Tax Price = Total Price / (1 + (Tax Rate / 100))**

*Example:* You paid $54.25 total. You know the local tax rate is 8.5%.
`$54.25 / 1.085 = $50.00`
The item cost exactly $50 before tax.

## Why Sales Tax Rates Vary So Much

In the US, there is no federal sales tax. Instead, sales tax is a combination of State, County, and City taxes. 
- **State Tax:** Imposed by the state government (e.g., California is 7.25%).
- **Local Tax:** Cities and counties can add their own "district taxes" on top of the state tax to fund local infrastructure (e.g., Los Angeles adds 2.25%).
- **Combined Rate:** The actual rate you pay at the register (e.g., in Los Angeles, you pay 9.5%).

Furthermore, different items are taxed differently. Groceries and prescription drugs are often tax-exempt, while prepared hot food at a restaurant is fully taxed. 

## FAQ

**What are the states with no sales tax?**
As of 2026, there are five states with no statewide sales tax: Alaska, Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, and Oregon. However, some localities in Alaska do charge a local sales tax.
