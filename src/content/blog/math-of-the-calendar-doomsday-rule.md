If someone asks you what day of the week you were born on, you probably have to look it up. But some people can answer almost instantly. How do they do it? 

Because the Gregorian calendar follows strict mathematical rules, determining the day of the week for any given date relies on a simple, albeit long, formula called **Zeller's Congruence** or the much faster mental trick known as the **Doomsday Algorithm**.

## The Doomsday Algorithm

Invented by the famous English mathematician John Conway, the Doomsday algorithm is a method that allows anyone to quickly calculate the day of the week in their head. 

It relies on the fact that every year has a specific "Doomsday." Certain easily memorable dates in the year always fall on this exact same day of the week.

### Step 1: Memorize the Key Doomsdays
In any given year, the following dates always fall on the exact same day of the week (the Doomsday):
- **4/4** (April 4)
- **6/6** (June 6)
- **8/8** (August 8)
- **10/10** (October 10)
- **12/12** (December 12)
- **Pi Day:** March 14 (3/14)
- **US Independence Day:** July 4 (7/4)
- **Halloween:** October 31 (10/31)

### Step 2: Find the Doomsday for the Current Year
Every year has an anchor day. For example:
- **2023:** Tuesday
- **2024 (Leap Year):** Thursday
- **2025:** Friday
- **2026:** Saturday

Because 2026's Doomsday is Saturday, that means April 4th, June 6th, August 8th, and Halloween are ALL Saturdays in 2026!

### Step 3: Count Forward or Backward
Once you know the anchor dates, calculating any other date is just simple addition or subtraction.

*Example:* What day of the week is **December 20, 2026**?
1. You know the 2026 Doomsday is Saturday.
2. You have memorized that **12/12** (December 12th) is a Doomsday. So, Dec 12th is a Saturday.
3. Dec 20th is exactly 8 days after Dec 12th.
4. 8 days is 1 week and 1 day.
5. Saturday + 1 day = **Sunday**.

December 20, 2026, is a Sunday. 

## The Complexities of the Calendar

The reason mental math algorithms are required is because our calendar is not neatly divisible by 7 (the number of days in a week).
- A standard year is 365 days. `365 / 7 = 52 weeks and 1 extra day.`
- This means dates shift forward exactly **one day of the week** every year. (If your birthday is on a Monday this year, it will be on a Tuesday next year).
- A leap year is 366 days. `366 / 7 = 52 weeks and 2 extra days.`
- This means leap years shift dates forward by **two days**.

## FAQ

**Did the calendar ever skip days?**
Yes! When the Catholic Church switched from the flawed Julian calendar to the highly precise Gregorian calendar in 1582, they had to correct centuries of drift. To realign the calendar with the solar equinox, they literally deleted 10 days. The day after Thursday, October 4, 1582, was Friday, October 15, 1582.
