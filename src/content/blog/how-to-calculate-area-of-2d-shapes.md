Area is the amount of 2D space inside the boundary of a flat object. Whether you are buying paint for a bedroom, laying sod in a backyard, or doing middle school math homework, knowing how to calculate area is an essential life skill.

Because shapes vary drastically, mathematicians have developed specific formulas for each geometric polygon.

## Squares and Rectangles

The most common and easiest shapes to calculate. Because their corners are perfect 90-degree right angles, you only need two measurements.

**Formula:** `Area = Length × Width` (or Base × Height)

**Example:** You want to carpet a bedroom that is 12 feet long and 10 feet wide.
`Area = 12 ft × 10 ft = 120 square feet.`

## Triangles

A triangle is mathematically exactly half of a rectangle. If you draw a diagonal line across a rectangle, you get two identical triangles. 

**Formula:** `Area = ½ × Base × Height`

**Example:** A triangular sail on a boat has a base of 6 meters and a height of 8 meters.
`Area = 0.5 × 6 × 8 = 24 square meters.`

*(Note: The 'Height' must be the perpendicular distance from the base to the opposite point, not the length of the slanted side).*

## Circles

Circles are unique because they don't have straight sides. To calculate the area of a circle, we must use the mathematical constant Pi (π), which is approximately 3.14159.

**Formula:** `Area = π × r²`
Where **r** is the radius (the distance from the exact center of the circle to the outer edge).

**Example:** You are building a circular patio with a radius of 5 feet.
1. Square the radius: `5 × 5 = 25`
2. Multiply by Pi: `3.14159 × 25 = 78.54`
`Area = 78.54 square feet.`

## Irregular Shapes (The Deconstruction Method)

Very rarely is a room or a backyard a perfect square or a perfect circle. Real-world spaces are usually irregular polygons. To find the area of an irregular shape, you must use the "Deconstruction Method."

1. **Break it down:** Draw imaginary lines to break the complex shape into standard, simple shapes (like breaking an L-shaped room into two separate rectangles).
2. **Calculate individually:** Find the area of each of the smaller shapes using the standard formulas.
3. **Add them together:** The total area is the sum of the individual areas.

## FAQ

**What is the difference between Area and Perimeter?**
Area measures the flat space *inside* a shape (measured in square units). Perimeter measures the length of the outside *boundary* or fence around the shape (measured in linear units).

**How do I calculate the area of a room if the measurements are in inches and feet?**
You must convert all measurements to a single unit before multiplying. A room that is 10 feet 6 inches by 12 feet must be calculated as `10.5 ft × 12.0 ft` to get an accurate square footage reading.
