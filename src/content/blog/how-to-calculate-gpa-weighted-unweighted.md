Your Grade Point Average (GPA) is a single, standardized number that represents your overall academic performance. High schools and universities use it heavily to determine college admissions, scholarship eligibility, and academic standing.

While finding the average of a few numbers is simple, finding a GPA is complex because not all classes are worth the same amount of "credit." 

## Understanding the Standard 4.0 Scale

The foundation of the GPA calculation in the United States is the 4.0 scale. Every letter grade you earn is converted into a numerical value (often called Quality Points).

- A = 4.0
- B = 3.0
- C = 2.0
- D = 1.0
- F = 0.0

*Note: Many schools use plus/minus grading, where an A- is 3.7 and a B+ is 3.3. Check your school's specific transcript rubric.*

## The GPA Formula

A GPA is a mathematically **weighted average**. You cannot simply add your grades together and divide by the number of classes. You must factor in how many *Credits* (or Credit Hours) each class is worth.

**GPA = (Total Quality Points) / (Total Attempted Credits)**

To find Total Quality Points, you multiply the numerical grade by the credit hours of the class.

### Step-by-Step Example

Imagine a college student takes three classes:
1. Biology (4 Credits) - Earned an A (4.0)
2. English (3 Credits) - Earned a B (3.0)
3. Physical Education (1 Credit) - Earned a C (2.0)

**Step 1: Calculate Quality Points for Each Class**
- Bio: 4.0 x 4 credits = **16 points**
- English: 3.0 x 3 credits = **9 points**
- PE: 2.0 x 1 credit = **2 points**

**Step 2: Find Total Quality Points and Total Credits**
- Total Quality Points: 16 + 9 + 2 = **27**
- Total Credits Attempted: 4 + 3 + 1 = **8**

**Step 3: Apply the Formula**
`27 / 8 = 3.375`

The student's GPA is **3.38**. Notice how the 'C' in the 1-credit PE class barely pulled down the GPA, while the 'A' in the heavy 4-credit Biology class pulled it up significantly. This is the power of a weighted average.

## Weighted vs. Unweighted GPA (High School)

In high school, students often encounter GPAs higher than 4.0. How is this possible?

- **Unweighted GPA:** Every class is graded on the standard 4.0 scale regardless of difficulty.
- **Weighted GPA:** Schools artificially inflate the scale to reward students for taking difficult classes (like Honors, AP, or IB courses). Usually, a 1.0 point "bump" is added.

Under a weighted system, an 'A' in standard English is worth 4.0, but an 'A' in AP English is worth **5.0**. This allows top-performing students taking rigorous course loads to achieve GPAs like 4.5 or 4.8. 

## FAQ

**Do colleges look at weighted or unweighted GPA?**
When evaluating applications, most university admissions offices will strip away your high school's unique weighting system and recalculate your core GPA on an unweighted 4.0 scale to standardize applications. However, they will simultaneously look at your "Course Rigor" to ensure you took challenging classes.
