If you are a freelancer, contractor, or hourly employee, you do not have a guaranteed annual salary. If you do not work, you do not get paid.

This makes budgeting for a mortgage or a car loan extremely difficult. To create a financial plan, you must accurately convert your fluctuating hourly wage into a reliable "Annual Salary" projection.

## The Standard Calculation

The baseline formula to convert an hourly wage into an annual salary relies on the standard American work year: **2,080 hours** (40 hours a week × 52 weeks).

**The Formula: `Hourly Rate × 2080 = Gross Annual Salary`**

If you make $25 an hour:
`$25 × 2080 = $52,000 a year.`

*The Mental Shortcut:* To quickly estimate an hourly-to-salary conversion in your head, simply double your hourly rate and add three zeros. (Double 25 = 50. Add three zeros = $50,000. It is a very close estimate!).

## The Danger of Unpaid Time Off

The 2080 formula assumes you will work 40 hours a week for 52 consecutive weeks without ever getting sick, taking a vacation, or experiencing a major holiday. 

If you are an hourly worker who does not receive Paid Time Off (PTO) benefits, using the 2080 multiplier will dangerously overinflate your budget. You must calculate your **Adjusted Hours**.

### Calculating Adjusted Hours
Let's assume a realistic year where you take two weeks of vacation, miss one week due to illness, and your office is closed for 5 standard federal holidays (which equals 1 more work week).

You are missing exactly **4 weeks** of paid work.
`52 weeks - 4 weeks = 48 weeks actually worked.`

Now, find your Adjusted Hours:
`48 weeks × 40 hours = 1,920 hours.`

### The Adjusted Salary Calculation
Let's run the $25/hour math again using your realistic schedule:
`$25 × 1920 = $48,000 a year.`

By simply taking standard sick time and holidays, your actual annual income dropped by **$4,000**. If you had signed a lease for an apartment based on the $52,000 projection, you would be struggling to pay rent in December.

## Accounting for Overtime

Conversely, if your job offers consistent overtime, the 2080 rule will severely *underestimate* your income.

Under the FLSA, any hours worked over 40 in a single workweek must be paid at "Time and a Half" (1.5× your base rate).

If you make $20 an hour, your standard salary projection is **$41,600**.
If you consistently work 45 hours a week:
- 40 hours at base rate: `$20 × 40 = $800 a week`.
- 5 hours at overtime rate ($30): `$30 × 5 = $150 a week`.
- Total weekly pay: `$950`.
- Projected Annual Salary: `$950 × 52 = $49,400`.

Just 5 hours of overtime a week increased your annual salary by almost $8,000!

## FAQ

**Do bonuses count toward my hourly rate?**
No. Bonuses and commissions are considered supplemental income and should never be factored into your baseline hourly-to-salary mathematical projections. Because they are not guaranteed, relying on them for your budget is financially dangerous. Always budget based on your base pay.
