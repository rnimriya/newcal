In the United States, tipping is a cultural expectation. Because the federal minimum wage for tipped employees (like waitstaff and bartenders) is legally set at a shockingly low $2.13 per hour, restaurant workers rely almost entirely on percentage-based gratuities to earn a living.

While the cultural standard for good service has shifted over the years from 15% to 20%, calculating these percentages at the end of a long dinner can be frustrating.

## Do You Tip Before or After Tax?

This is one of the most highly debated mathematical questions in dining. 
Do you calculate the 20% tip based on the Subtotal (the actual cost of the food), or on the Grand Total (the cost of the food plus the government sales tax)?

**The Etiquette Rule:** Etiquette experts (like the Emily Post Institute) universally state that gratuity should be calculated on the **pre-tax subtotal**. You are tipping the server for their service regarding the food; you do not need to tip the server on the taxes the government is charging you.

However, many point-of-sale systems (the electronic tablets handed to you at the table) are programmed to calculate their "Suggested Tip" percentages based on the Post-Tax Grand Total, mathematically inflating the tip amount.

## Mental Math Shortcuts

If you don't have a calculator handy, you can easily calculate perfect tips using the "Move the Decimal" trick. 

Because we use a Base-10 numbering system, finding 10% of any number is mathematically effortless. You simply move the decimal point one place to the left. 

Let's assume your restaurant bill is **$64.00**.
Move the decimal one spot to the left. 
**10% = $6.40.**

Once you know what 10% is, you can calculate anything else instantly.

### How to Calculate a 20% Tip
Since 20% is just 10% doubled, simply double the number you just found.
- 10% is `$6.40`.
- Double it: `$6.40 + $6.40 = $12.80`.
A perfect 20% tip is **$12.80**.

### How to Calculate a 15% Tip
15% is simply 10% plus half of 10% (which equals 5%).
- 10% is `$6.40`.
- Half of that (5%) is `$3.20`.
- Add them together: `$6.40 + $3.20 = $9.60`.
A perfect 15% tip is **$9.60**.

### How to Calculate an 18% Tip
18% is a common middle-ground tip. The easiest way to calculate it in your head is to find 20%, and then subtract roughly a dollar or two.
- 20% is `$12.80`.
- Subtract roughly $1.30 to represent the missing 2%.
- The tip is roughly **$11.50**.

## The Psychology of Tipping Math

Studies show that consumers who do not understand percentages are highly susceptible to "anchoring" tactics. 

When a coffee shop hands you an electronic screen with suggested tips of "20%, 25%, or 30%", your brain anchors to 25% as the "normal" middle option. If the screen had suggested "10%, 15%, or 20%", your brain would anchor to 15%. 

By understanding the math and calculating the tip yourself, you protect yourself from psychological anchoring and ensure you are tipping fairly based on your own standards.

## FAQ

**How do I tip when using a gift card or a coupon?**
You must always calculate the tip based on the **original price** of the food, before the discount was applied. The server did the exact same amount of physical work carrying the food to your table, regardless of whether the restaurant's marketing department decided to give you a 50% off coupon.
