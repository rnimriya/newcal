When evaluating a job offer, employers love to quote large annual salaries. "We will pay you $75,000 a year!" sounds fantastic. But if that job requires you to work 50 hours a week, is it actually a better deal than a job paying $60,000 that only requires 35 hours a week?

To accurately compare job offers, you must break the annual salary down into its fundamental unit: the **Hourly Wage**.

## The Baseline Conversion (The 2080 Rule)

In the United States, the mathematical standard for a full-time work year is **2,080 hours**.
This is based on working 40 hours a week for all 52 weeks of the year (`40 × 52 = 2,080`).

To find your baseline gross hourly wage, divide your salary by 2,080.
**Formula: `Salary / 2080 = Hourly Rate`**

*Example:* `$75,000 / 2080 = $36.05 per hour.`

## The Problem with Salaried Exempt Status

The 2080 rule only works if you actually work exactly 40 hours a week. However, under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), most salaried employees are "Exempt" from overtime pay. This means you get paid exactly $75,000 regardless of whether you work 40 hours or 60 hours.

If you consistently work 50-hour weeks, your math changes:
`50 hours × 52 weeks = 2,600 hours a year.`
`$75,000 / 2600 = $28.84 per hour.`

By working an extra 10 hours a week, you mathematically gave yourself a massive pay cut, dropping your true hourly rate by over $7 an hour!

## Factoring in Paid Time Off (PTO)

What if you take a two-week vacation? Does that change your hourly rate?
If you are salaried with **Paid Time Off (PTO)**, your hourly rate does not change. The company is literally paying you your hourly rate while you sit on the beach. You are still being compensated for 2,080 hours.

However, if you are a contractor on a fixed yearly contract with *unpaid* time off, taking a two-week vacation reduces your total working hours to 2,000 (`50 weeks × 40 hours`).

## Gross Hourly vs. Net Hourly

The numbers we calculated above are your **Gross** hourly wages (before taxes). But you can't pay your rent with Gross wages. You pay rent with **Net** (take-home) wages.

If you make $75,000, you are likely in the 22% federal tax bracket, plus FICA (7.65%), plus state taxes. Let's assume you lose roughly 25% of your paycheck to taxes.

**Calculating Net Hourly Wage:**
1. Calculate Gross Hourly: `$75,000 / 2080 = $36.05`
2. Multiply by the percentage you keep (75%): `$36.05 × 0.75 = $27.03`

Every hour you sit at your desk, you are putting exactly **$27.03** of usable cash into your bank account. 

## FAQ

**Why do companies prefer to pay salaries instead of hourly wages?**
Predictability and cost control. For a company, a salary is a fixed mathematical expense that makes annual budgeting incredibly easy. Furthermore, by making employees Exempt, the company avoids the catastrophic, unpredictable costs of paying time-and-a-half overtime during busy seasons.
